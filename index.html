About the Workshop

Soft robotics has emerged as a transformative field, offering unique advantages in human-robot interaction, adaptability, and safe operation in unstructured environments. However, the field faces significant challenges in reproducibility across three critical domains: manufacturing, testing, and simulation.

This workshop brings together researchers, practitioners, and industry experts to address the fundamental question: How can we establish reproducible practices in soft robotics that enable reliable comparison of results, validation of findings, and acceleration of scientific progress?

By focusing on the interconnected challenges of fabrication variability, standardized testing protocols, and simulation-to-reality gaps, we aim to develop a roadmap for making soft robotics a more rigorous and reproducible scientific discipline.

Topics of Interest

We welcome contributions addressing, but not limited to, the following topics:

Manufacturing & Fabrication

  • Reproducible fabrication protocols for soft actuators and sensors
  • Material characterization and standardization
  • Quality control and consistency in soft robot manufacturing
  • Open-source fabrication methods and documentation
  • Batch-to-batch variability analysis

Testing & Benchmarking

  • Standardized testing protocols for soft robots
  • Benchmark tasks and performance metrics
  • Experimental design for reproducible results
  • Uncertainty quantification in soft robotics experiments
  • Open datasets and experimental protocols

Simulation & Modeling

  • Validation of soft robot simulators
  • Sim-to-real transfer in soft robotics
  • Model parameter identification and validation
  • Multi-physics simulation frameworks
  • Benchmarking simulation tools

Cross-Cutting Themes

  • Open-source hardware and software for reproducibility
  • Data sharing and documentation best practices
  • Community standards and guidelines
  • Reproducibility in soft robot learning and control
  • Case studies of reproducibility challenges and solutions

Submission Guidelines

Call for Papers

Submission details will be announced soon. Please check back for updates.

We invite submissions in the following categories:

  • Full Papers: 6-8 pages describing original research, case studies, or position papers
  • Extended Abstracts: 2-4 pages describing work in progress, preliminary results, or novel ideas
  • Demonstrations: 2-page abstract describing a hardware or software demonstration

All submissions should follow the IEEE conference format. Accepted papers will be made available on the workshop website. We encourage authors to make their code, data, and fabrication files publicly available to promote reproducibility.
